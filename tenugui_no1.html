Tenugui

What is Tenugui?

Tenugui is a traditional cotten cloth used in Japan since the 8th century. These cloths can vary in size, but the most popular size measures around 34cm wide by 90cm long and bleached in various colors and designs.

Each Tenugui is carefully hand-dyed by craftsmen with a traditional dyeing technique called "Chusen". This technique allows the dyes to seep through the cloth in its entirely, resulting in vibrant hues and long lasting colors. The technique also allows the use of multiple colors at once, thereby making it easier to create complex designs using a wide range of colors.

The Tenugui has been loved by the Japanese for centuries and is still being used today. There is no "correct way" of using a Tenugui - you can use it as with any towel, for wiping objects, for wrapping, or even just to hang it on the wall as decoration. The almost unlimited use of the tenugui has made this one of the most popular household items in Japan.
